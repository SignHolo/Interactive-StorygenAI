# Prologue

## First Day at Heizen Mana Academy

The morning air above Heizen Mana Academy shimmered faintly with layered mana fields.

Students gathered in clusters across the vast stone courtyard, their auras brushing against one another like unseen currents. Some flared brightly with confidence. Others flickered with poorly controlled excitement.

At the academy gates stood a lone figure in a black cloak.

Messy silver hair.
Calm brown eyes.
A single katana resting at his left side.

Shiné.

A newcomer.

And more importantly — an outsider.

He was not born in Leisen.
He had only arrived in the kingdom several days ago.

Already, whispers had begun.

“Transfer student?”
“He doesn’t look like a mage.”
“His mana… is that it?”

A few students subtly expanded their aura perception.

What they sensed barely reached ordinary levels.

Around seven hundred.

Lower than most Gifted applicants.
Embarrassingly unimpressive for Heizen standards.

Interest faded almost instantly.

Shiné walked forward without reacting.

His Mana Core pulsed steadily — quiet, compressed, perfectly masked.

The vast courtyard felt small to him.

Twelve streams of thought flowed silently in parallel behind his calm expression.

Aura densities.
Core frequencies.
Instability points in nearby mana emissions.
Combat habits inferred from stance and breathing.

Information layered effortlessly.

He exhaled.

The academy’s central tower rose high above, engraved with ancient mana inscriptions that regulated the campus’ ambient field. He could feel the frequency modulation woven through the stone.

Impressive.

Refined.

But predictable.

Students moved toward the main hall for the entrance assembly.

Some glanced at the katana at his waist.

“A swordsman?”
“At Heizen?”

Mages dominated this academy.

Reinforcement specialists existed — but rarely stood out.

Shiné stepped across the engraved threshold.

For a brief moment, the academy’s detection array brushed against his aura.

It recorded him.

Low-tier.
Non-threatening.
Unremarkable.

Exactly as intended.

Unseen by the system, deep within him, an Arch-tier Mana Core rotated in absolute silence.

And somewhere else on campus—

Two overwhelming presences stirred faintly.

Ice.
Fire.

The first day at Heizen Mana Academy had begun.
